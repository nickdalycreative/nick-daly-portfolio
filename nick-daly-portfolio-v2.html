About

I am a researcher, creative practitioner, and institutional strategist working at the intersections of culture, scholarship, and public life.

My work spans academic research, curatorial projects, multimedia production, and institutional consulting. I'm driven by a commitment to making knowledge accessible and meaningful across diverse publics, and by the belief that creative and intellectual practices can generate genuine institutional transformation.

Over the years, I've collaborated with cultural organizations, universities, and public institutions on projects that range from digital archive design to public scholarship initiatives and strategic planning.

My scholarly work engages with visual culture, institutional theory, digital humanities, and the politics of knowledge production. I'm particularly interested in how cultural institutions can become more responsive, equitable, and innovative.
